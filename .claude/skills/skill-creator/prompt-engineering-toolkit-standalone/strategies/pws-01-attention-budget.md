### **Prompt Writing Strategy 1: Mastering the "Attention Budget" & Front-Loading the Narrative**

**Lesson:** The AI has a finite "Attention Budget," and it allocates the most focus to the concepts it encounters at the **beginning of the prompt**. To ensure a successful and coherent generation, all critical elements of the scene—the subject, the core action, and the setting—must be established immediately in a concise, factual "Pure Logline" that precedes the detailed descriptive paragraph.

**Rationale:** The AI builds a "mental scaffolding" for the scene based on the first few sentences. If the core concept is buried in details or revealed at the end, the AI may fail to grasp the scene's primary purpose, leading to ignored elements, confused compositions, or a loss of focus. This front-loading strategy gives the AI a clear blueprint from the very start, which it can then "flesh out" with the subsequent details.

---

#### **Principle A: Establish Everything Early (The "Pure Logline")**

**Goal:** A wide shot where Tony uses his spray can to create a bridge across a chasm.

* **INEFFECTIVE (Buried Lead):**  
  Graffito Mixed-Media Stop-Motion: Tony, a small puppet with a PHOTOGRAPHIC CUTOUT face etched with desperation, is the subject. The scene is a demolished overpass diorama, a massive structure of gritty PAINTED CARDBOARD. He stands on one crumbling edge, separated by a wide, dark chasm. He raises his spray can and, in a visceral stop-motion sequence, unleashes a burst of orange mist that congeals into a glowing, unstable-looking bridge.  
  * **Critique:** The core action—creating a bridge—is buried deep within the descriptive paragraph. The AI spends its initial "Attention Budget" on "Tony" and "overpass" and may not understand that the bridge creation is the most important event in the shot.  
*   
* **EFFECTIVE (Pure Logline Upfront):**  
  Graffito Mixed-Media Stop-Motion: \*\*Trapped on a demolished overpass, Tony uses a magic spray can to create a glowing orange bridge of paint across a wide chasm.\*\* Tony, a small puppet with a PHOTOGRAPHIC CUTOUT face etched with desperation, is the subject. The scene is a demolished overpass diorama...  
  * **Critique:** This is successful because the entire story of the shot is summarized in the first sentence. The AI immediately knows: **WHO** (Tony), **WHAT** (creates a bridge), **WHERE** (on an overpass), and **HOW** (with a spray can). The detailed paragraph now serves to add texture to this established, clear foundation.  
*

#### **Principle B: Avoid Last-Minute Reveals ("Heavy Second Half")**

**Goal:** A shot where a seemingly normal pile of trash suddenly explodes, revealing a supernatural swarm of pigeons.

* **INEFFECTIVE (Last-Minute Reveal):**  
  Graffito Mixed-Media Stop-Motion: The scene is a desolate schoolyard diorama made of cracked PAINTED CARDBOARD. The lighting is a harsh, high-noon sun. In the center of the yard is a large, dense mass of what appears to be discarded paper debris, made from torn and CRUMPLED PAPER. The shot is completely still, holding on the quiet, empty yard. The trash pile suddenly detonates upwards and outwards in a violent, supernatural explosion of pigeon wings and feathers.  
  * **Critique:** The most important and visually dramatic event—the explosion—is tacked on at the very end. The AI has already spent its "Attention Budget" building a "quiet, still, empty yard with trash." This heavy, last-minute ask can easily be ignored or result in a weak, poorly-rendered effect.  
*   
* **EFFECTIVE (Reveal is Stated Upfront):**  
  Graffito Mixed-Media Stop-Motion: \*\*A mass of what appears to be discarded paper debris suddenly detonates upwards in a supernatural explosion of pigeon wings and feathers, completely obliterating the sky.\*\* The scene is a handcrafted schoolyard diorama. First, the schoolyard is still, with the dense mass of debris resting in the center. Next, this mass suddenly detonates...  
  * **Critique:** This works because the "surprise" is revealed to the AI in the Pure Logline. The AI knows from the start that the ultimate goal is an "explosion of wings and feathers." It can then properly allocate its resources to render both the initial quiet and the final, chaotic eruption as part of a single, coherent narrative sequence.

#### **Principle C: Be Mindful of the Token Budget (Avoid Dilution)**

**Goal:** A simple, powerful close-up of Tony's terrified face being hit by strobing red and blue lights.

* **INEFFECTIVE (Overly Verbose / Diluted Focus):**  
  Graffito Mixed-Media Stop-Motion: An extreme close-up on the terrified face of Tony. The subject is Tony, whose PHOTOGRAPHIC CUTOUT face is a specific high-resolution print from a 1970s-era photograph, showing a child with hazel-green eyes and curly brown hair common to Italian-American youths of the period. The scene is a pure black void, representing the absence of discernible space, a psychological landscape. The lighting comes from an unseen, off-camera police car, likely a 1974 Dodge Monaco, its rooftop light bar flashing in a standard alternating pattern, casting beams of pure red (approximately 620 nm wavelength) and pure blue (approximately 475 nm wavelength) light that sweep across his features with a specific rotational speed of 150 RPM.  
  * **Critique:** The prompt is cluttered with excessive, technically irrelevant details (the make of the car, the wavelength of the light). This flood of tokens dilutes the "Attention Budget," forcing the AI to waste focus on unimportant concepts and weakening its ability to render the core idea: a terrified face and strobing lights.  
*   
* **EFFECTIVE (Concise & Focused):**  
  Graffito Mixed-Media Stop-Motion: In an extreme close-up, Tony's terrified face is painted by aggressive, off-camera beams of strobing red and blue light in a pure black void. The subject is Tony's face, a \*\*PHOTOGRAPHIC CUTOUT\*\* locked in an expression of pure, anxious terror. The scene is a pure black, featureless void. The lighting is the entire focus of the scene: aggressive, \*\*off-camera red and blue beams of light\*\* relentlessly sweep and strobe across his features, casting sharp, whipping shadows. The shot is a powerful, cinematic portrait of sensory overload.  
  * **Critique:** This is successful because every word is chosen for maximum impact. It focuses entirely on what is visually and emotionally important—the face, the void, the quality and color of the light, and the feeling it creates—without wasting a single token on unnecessary information.
