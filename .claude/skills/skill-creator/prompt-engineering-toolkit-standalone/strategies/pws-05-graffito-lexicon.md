### **Prompt Writing Strategy 5: The "Graffito" Lexicon (Material & Motion Translation)**

**Lesson:** All subjects, scenes, and effects must be described not as they are in reality, but as handcrafted, tangible props being animated in a stop-motion film. This is achieved by consistently applying the project's established **Material Lexicon** and **Motion Lexicon**. This is the cornerstone of our stylistic integrity.

**Rationale:** The LoRA has been trained to associate our unique visual style with a specific vocabulary of arts-and-crafts materials and puppet-like movements. Consistently using this lexicon is the primary way we "trigger" the LoRA to render scenes in the "Graffito" style. Describing an object as "a car" is a generic instruction; describing it as "a car prop made of **PAINTED CARDBOARD**" is a specific, stylistic command.

---

#### **Principle A: Material Lexicon (The "How Would They Build It?" Test)**

**Goal:** Translate any real-world object into a plausible, handcrafted "Graffito" prop.

* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "A vintage television set."  
* **USE THIS (Material Translation):** "A vintage TV prop, its main casing a large box made of \*\*PAINTED CARDBOARD\*\* with a faux wood grain texture. The screen is a glossy \*\*PHOTOGRAPHIC CUTOUT\*\* of a static-filled image, and its dials are painted bottle caps."  
* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "A slice of pizza."  
* **USE THIS (Material Translation):** "A pizza slice prop, its crust a triangle of \*\*PAINTED CARDBOARD\*\*. The sauce is a thick layer of glossy \*\*THICK IMPASTO PAINT\*\*, and the melted cheese is a stringy, gooey-looking application of melted and re-hardened resin."  
* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "The rain is pouring."  
* **USE THIS (Material Translation):** "The pouring rain is a continuous special effect, a dense, visible curtain of individual \*\*tiny blue cellophane droplets\*\* falling straight down."

---

#### **Principle B: Motion Lexicon (The "How Would They Animate It?" Test)**

**Goal:** Describe any action as if it were being performed by a physical puppet, frame by frame.

* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "The man turns his head."  
* **USE THIS (Motion Translation):** "The man reacts with a \*\*slow, deliberate turn of his head\*\*, his neck moving with a stiff, \*\*segmented\*\* quality."  
* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "The doors burst open."  
* **USE THIS (Motion Translation):** "The doors violently \*\*burst outwards\*\* off their hinges with a single, explosive \*\*stop-motion jerk\*\*."  
* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "The pigeon flies."  
* **USE THIS (Motion Translation):** "The pigeon flies across the frame, its articulated paper wings beating with a powerful, \*\*jerky stop-motion rhythm\*\*."

**Lesson:** All objects, characters, and effects in the "Graffito" universe must be described as tangible, handcrafted props built from a specific "Arts & Crafts" material and motion lexicon. This is the cornerstone of the entire visual style.

**Rationale:** The LoRA has been trained on a specific set of textures and materials. Using these reserved tokens (PAINTED CARDBOARD, THICK IMPASTO PAINT, jerky, wobble, etc.) is the most reliable way to activate the "Graffito" style. For new objects or actions, we must ask the **"How Would They Build It?"** or **"How Would They Animate It?"** test, translating them into their plausible, handcrafted counterparts.

**Few-Shot Examples (Materiality):**

* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "A city bus drives down the street."  
* **USE THIS (Graffito Lexicon):** "A city bus prop, its body constructed from \*\*LAYERS OF PAINTED CARDBOARD\*\*, drives down the street."  
* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "A fire hydrant explodes with water."  
* **USE THIS (Graffito Lexicon):** "A fire hydrant prop explodes, unleashing a chaotic plume of torn \*\*CRUMPLED CELLOPHANE STRIPS\*\* and wisps of \*\*PAINTED COTTON BATTING\*\* smoke."

**Few-Shot Examples (Motion):**

* **INSTEAD OF (Generic):** "The dog walks."  
* **USE THIS (Graffito Lexicon):** "The dog moves with slow, \*\*heavy-footed, jerky stop-motion steps\*\*."
