# Pillar III: The Art Direction

> **SCOPE:** Visual style, prompting tokens, materials, and lighting.
> **USE WHEN:** Generating Img2Vid prompts or describing visuals.

## 1. The Prompting Engine (Strict Syntax)

**Trigger Phrase:** `Clay-Plastic Pose-to-Pose Animation —`

**Formula:** `[Trigger] [Subject] [Motion] in [Setting]. [Lighting].`

## 2. Materiality & Shader

- **The Surface:** `SMOOTH CLAY-PLASTIC SHADERS`.
- **The Constraint:** **NO TEXTURE.** No grunge, no grit, no realism. The world is a "Pristine Toy Aesthetic."
- **Edges:** `CLEAN SILHOUETTES` with distinct value separation.
- **Geometry:** `SIMPLIFIED GRAPHIC GEOMETRY`. Backgrounds are painted planes/shapes projected in 3D.

## 3. The Lighting Palettes (Binary System)

### DAY (The Setup)
- *Vibe:* Calm, Safe, Pastel.
- *Tokens:* `WARM PASTEL PALETTE`, `Soft Global Illumination`, `Diffuse Bloom`, `Bright Blue Sky`, `Single Large Circular Disc (Sun)`.

### NIGHT (The Scheme)
- *Vibe:* Dangerous, Saturated, Neon.
- *Tokens:* `COOL SATURATED PALETTE`, `Indigo Sky`, `Magenta Rim Lighting`, `Pink Crescent Moon`, `High-Contrast`.

## 4. Asset Rules (Toy Logic)

- **Wood:** "Clean light brown geometry" (No grain).
- **Rope:** "Thick GREEN rope."
- **Straps:** "Bright BLUE bands."
- **Liquids:** "Viscous, thick blobs" (Tar/Paint).

## 5. Cinematic Punctuation

- **Crash Zoom:** Violent camera movement for impact.
- **Whip Pan:** Rapid lateral blur to hide cuts.
- **Grotesque Close-Up:** Detailed, uncomfortable zoom on facial features (bloodshot eyes).
- **Graphic Onomatopoeia:** Words like "BOOM!" or "CRASH!" appearing as **physical 3D objects** in the frame. (Note: These are Visual FX, not speech bubbles).
